# Interview Preparation & Experience Sharing

A personal collection of interview preparation materials, behavioral answers, and experience notes for software engineering interviews.

## Contents

### `introduction/`
- **introduction.txt** — Self-introduction script and STAR-format behavioral answers covering common topics:
  - Tell me about yourself
  - Delivering results and overcoming obstacles
  - Handling disagreements with manager/teammates
  - Receiving and acting on critical feedback
- **handy conversion.txt** — Quick reference for system design scale estimates (requests/sec to requests/month)

### `OKX_Interview_Guide_Final.pdf`
Comprehensive interview guide for OKX, covering technical and behavioral rounds.

## How to Use

1. **Behavioral prep** — Read through `introduction/introduction.txt` and adapt the STAR stories to your own experience. Practice saying them out loud until they feel natural.
2. **System design** — Keep `handy conversion.txt` handy when estimating scale; knowing that 400 RPS ≈ 1B requests/month saves mental math during interviews.
3. **Company-specific** — Review the OKX guide before interviewing at crypto/fintech companies; many of the patterns apply broadly.

## Tips

- Quantify impact wherever possible (e.g., "reduced API latency by 40%", "reduced production bugs by 95%").
- For behavioral questions, lead with the situation briefly and spend most time on your actions and results.
- System design: always clarify scale requirements early — use the conversion guide to anchor your estimates.
